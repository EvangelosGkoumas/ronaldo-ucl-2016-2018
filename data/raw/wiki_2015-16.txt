The '''2015–16 season''' was '''[[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid Club de Fútbol]]'s''' [[List of Real Madrid CF seasons|112th season]] in existence and the club's [[La Liga records and statistics#All-time table|85th consecutive season]] in the top flight of [[Football in Spain|Spanish football]]. It covered a period from 1 July 2015 to 30 June 2016.

Under new manager [[Rafael Benítez]], Real Madrid remained unbeaten in the [[2015–16 La Liga|league]] until a 3–2 loss at [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] on the matchday 11. Perhaps, his reign is best remembered by multiple lopsided wins achieved both in La Liga and the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] (6–0 vs [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]], 8–0 vs [[Malmö FF|Malmö]], 10–2 vs [[Rayo Vallecano]] and others). In the [[2015–16 Copa del Rey#Round of 32|Copa del Rey round of 32]], Real accidentally fielded an ineligible player, who was suspended from the previous season (when he was representing a different team), resulting in the team's controversial disqualification from the tournament by the competition judge despite protests from President [[Florentino Pérez|Pérez]].<ref name="mr 2"/> In the meantime, Real comfortably topped their [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League group stage#Group A|UCL group]] with 16 points and a +16 goal difference. Benítez was relieved of his duties mid-season, following allegations of unpopularity with supporters, displeasure with players and a failure to get good results against top teams.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/zidane-a-club-legend-in-the-real-madrid-dugout|title=Zidane: a club legend in the Real Madrid dugout|publisher=realmadrid.com|date=4 January 2016|access-date=4 January 2016}}</ref> Benítez's departure was announced along with the promotion of [[Zinedine Zidane]] to his first head coaching role.<ref name="Coach Zidane">{{cite web | url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/0229-0e930f484246-2c717a89d01b-1000--zidane-replaces-benitez-at-real-madrid/ | title=Zidane replaces Benítez at Real Madrid | website = [[UEFA]] | access-date=5 January 2016}}</ref> Under Zidane, Madrid managed to turn the odds in its favor, ultimately winning the Champions League, something no one expected. The notable results include a 2–1 away victory over reigning [[Treble (association football)|treble]] winners [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]], who were on a record-breaking winning streak, a fantastic comeback against [[VfL Wolfsburg|Wolfsburg]] in the Champions League quarter-finals (after losing the away game 0–2, Madrid erased the deficit and won 3–0 at home, courtesy of a [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] hat-trick), as well as a 12-game winning streak to conclude the league campaign, meaning Real finished second, with 90 points and just one point behind champions Barcelona, coming agonizingly close to clinching the title and overcoming a 12-point deficit in the process.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/la-liga-table/2015|work=Sky Sports|title=2015–16 La Liga Table|date=8 June 2016 |access-date=15 June 2017}}</ref> Finally, on 28 May, Real Madrid's eleventh Champions League title was won thanks to a 5–3 penalty shoot-out victory over rivals [[Atlético Madrid]] after a 1–1 draw in the [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final|final]], with the achievement being termed "''La Undécima''".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/05/1-1-la-undecima-is-ours|title=1–1: La Undécima is ours|publisher=Real Madrid|access-date=28 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/022d-0e957c5979eb-37493cb53cd0-1000--spot-on-real-madrid-defeat-atletico-in-final-again/|title=Spot-on Real Madrid defeat Atlético in final again|date=28 May 2016|website=[[UEFA]]}}</ref><ref>[https://underthecoshblog.com/2016/06/06/real-madrid-201516-season-review/ Real Madrid 2015/16 Season Review: Real Madrid 2015/16 Season Review:]</ref> 

This season was for the first time since [[1998–99 Real Madrid CF season|1998–99]] not featuring the all-time legendary goalkeeper [[Iker Casillas]], who after 16 years of his service with Los Merengues, bidded farewell to later on join [[FC Porto|Porto]], and the first since [[2009–10 Real Madrid CF season|2009–10]] without [[Sami Khedira]], who departed to [[Juventus]].

==Season overview==
===Pre-season===
''[[2014 UEFA Champions League Final|La Décima]]'' winning coach [[Carlo Ancelotti]] left the club on 25 May 2015, after winning two out of possible six trophies in the [[2014–15 Real Madrid CF season|2014–15 season]] and narrowly missing out both on the league and Champions League titles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/05/florentino-perez-holds-a-press-conference|title=Florentino Pérez holds a press-conference|date=25 May 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.}}</ref> He was then replaced by [[Rafael Benítez]] on 3 June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/06/official-announcement-rafa-benitez|title=Official announcement: Rafa Benítez|date=3 June 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.}}</ref>

Brazilian duo [[Casemiro]] and [[Danilo (footballer, born July 1991)|Danilo]] were bought from [[FC Porto|Porto]] for a fee of €7.5&nbsp;million and €31.5&nbsp;million respectively. Both signed long-term deals with the club, with Casemiro returning after Real Madrid exercised their buy-back clause option on the player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcporto.pt/pt/noticias/Pages/Casemiro-Real-Madrid-FC-Porto-05-06-15.aspx|title=Real Madrid exerceu opção de resgate de Casemiro|date=5 June 2015|work=fcporto.pt}}</ref> Spanish wingers [[Marco Asensio]] and [[Lucas Vázquez]] also joined the club, while [[Sami Khedira]] signed for [[Juventus F.C.|Juventus]] after his contract was not renewed by the club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/jun/09/juventus-sami-khedira-real-madrid-free-transfer|title=Juventus sign Sami Khedira from Real Madrid on free transfer|date=9 June 2015|access-date=9 June 2015|work=The Guardian}}</ref> [[Javier Hernández]] soon departed back to [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] after Madrid did not make his season-long loan from the last campaign a permanent deal.

The contracts of [[Dani Carvajal]], [[Marcelo (footballer, born 1988)|Marcelo]] and [[Sergio Ramos]] were renewed for another six years and were set to expire in the summer of 2020.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/07/official-statement-carvajal|title=Official Statement: Carvajal|date=8 July 2015|access-date=8 July 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/07/official-announcement-marcelo|title=Official Statement: Carvajal|date=10 July 2015|access-date=10 July 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/sergio-ramos-signs-his-contract-extension-with-real-madrid|title=Sergio Ramos signs his contract extension with Real Madrid|date=17 August 2015|access-date=17 August 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.}}</ref> [[Pepe (footballer, born 1983)|Pepe]] extended his contract until 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-pepe|title=Official Announcement: Pepe|date=21 August 2015|access-date=21 August 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.}}</ref> Casemiro's contract was extended to 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-casemiro|title=Official announcement: Casemiro|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=31 August 2015|access-date=31 August 2015}}</ref>

[[Iker Casillas]] left the club on 11 July 2015 and was transferred to Porto after 16 years with ''Los Blancos''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Official Statement: Iker Casillas|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/07/official-statement-iker-casillas|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=11 July 2015|date=11 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/33455332|title=Iker Casillas to join Porto from Real Madrid|date=11 July 2015|access-date=11 July 2015|publisher=BBC}}</ref> [[Kiko Casilla]] was signed as a replacement on 17 July 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kiko Casilla, traspassat al R. Madrid|url=http://www.rcdespanyol.com/ca/noticies-primer-equip/kiko-casilla-traspassat-al-r-madrid/_n:870/|work=rcdespanyol.com|access-date=17 July 2015|date=17 July 2015}}</ref>

The schedule of the season was announced on 14 July 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Calendario completo del Real Madrid|url=http://www.laliga.es/noticias/calendario-completo-del-real-madrid|work=laliga.es|access-date=14 July 2015|date=14 July 2015}}</ref>

On 18 August 2015, [[Mateo Kovačić]] was signed from [[Inter Milan|Internazionale]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inter.it/en/news/48790|title=Mancini: "We had our chances"|date=16 August 2015|publisher=inter.it|access-date=18 August 2015|archive-date=29 February 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200229183658/https://www.inter.it/en/news/48790|url-status=dead}}</ref> while [[Marco Asensio]] was loaned to [[RCD Espanyol|Espanyol]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rcdespanyol.com/es/actualidad/marco-asensio-nueva-incorporacion/_n:1040/|title=Marco Asensio, nueva incorporación|date=20 August 2015|publisher=rcdespanyol.com|access-date=20 August 2015}}</ref>

===August===
Madrid started off their [[2015–16 La Liga|2015–16]] league campaign with a goalless draw at [[Sporting de Gijón]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/0-0-real-madrid-deserved-victory-at-el-molinon|title=0–0: Real Madrid deserved victory at El Molinón|date=23 August 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=23 August 2015}}</ref>

[[Asier Illarramendi]] was sold to [[Real Sociedad]] on 26 August 2015, while [[Fábio Coentrão]] was loaned to [[AS Monaco FC|Monaco]] and [[Lucas Silva (footballer, born 1993)|Lucas Silva]] to [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-illarramendi|title=Official Announcement: Illarramendi|date=26 August 2015|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=26 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-coentrao|title=Official Announcement: Coentrão|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=26 August 2015|access-date=26 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/official-announcement-lucas-silva|title=Official Announcement: Lucas Silva|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=27 August 2015|access-date=27 August 2015}}</ref>

Madrid thrashed [[Real Betis]] in their home opener by a score of 5–0 after braces from [[Gareth Bale]] and [[James Rodríguez]] and a goal from [[Karim Benzema]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/08/5-0-spectacular-display-of-firepower-in-the-bernabeus-la-liga-opener|title=5–0: Spectacular display of firepower in the Bernabéu's La Liga opener|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=30 August 2015|access-date=30 August 2015}}</ref>

===September===
[[Cristiano Ronaldo]] scored five goals and had an assist for Benzema in a 6–0 away rout of Espanyol.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/09/0-6-real-madrid-in-exhibition-form-as-cristiano-ronaldo-scores-five-goals|title=0–6: Real Madrid in exhibition form as Cristiano Ronaldo scores five goals|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=12 September 2015|access-date=12 September 2015}}</ref> With the five goals, Ronaldo set his Spanish league goal tally to 230 goals in 203 games, surpassing [[Raúl (footballer)|Raúl]]'s record of 228 goals in 550 games for Real Madrid in La Liga, becoming the all-time top scorer for Real Madrid in the Spanish league.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laliga.es/noticias/un-historico-ronaldo-lidera-la-goleada-al-espanyol|title=Un histórico Ronaldo lidera la goleada al Espanyol|trans-title=A historic Ronaldo leads the thrashing at Espanyol|publisher=laliga.es|date=12 September 2015|access-date=12 September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com/real-madrid/story/2607506/cristiano-ronaldo-sets-real-madrid-la-liga-scoring-record|title=Cristiano Ronaldo becomes Real Madrid's all-time La Liga top scorer|publisher=ESPN|date=12 September 2015|access-date=12 September 2015}}</ref>

Real started the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League]] season with a 4–0 home victory over [[FC Shakhtar Donetsk|Shakhtar Donetsk]]. Benzema scored the opener and Ronaldo had yet another hat-trick just four days after his last.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/09/4-0-real-madrid-thrash-shakhtar-with-a-hat-trick-from-ronaldo|title=4–0: Real Madrid thrash Shakhtar with a hat trick from Ronaldo|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=15 September 2015|access-date=15 September 2015}}</ref>

A lone goal by Benzema lifted Madrid over [[Granada CF|Granada]] to maintain their winning streak.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/09/1-0-benzema-grabs-winner-against-granada-to-seal-fourth-straight-victory|title=1–0: Benzema grabs winner against Granada to seal fourth straight victory|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=19 September 2015|access-date=19 September 2015}}</ref>

Benzema scored a brace to help Real record another victory at [[Athletic Bilbao]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/09/1-2-a-benzema-brace-puts-real-madrid-top-of-la-liga|title=1–2: A Benzema brace puts Real Madrid top of La Liga|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=23 September 2015|access-date=23 September 2015}}</ref>

Against [[Málaga CF|Málaga]], Madrid came out with a goalless draw.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/09/0-0-real-madrid-deserved-to-win-against-malaga|title=0–0: Real Madrid deserved to win against Málaga|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=26 September 2015|access-date=26 September 2015}}</ref>

===October===
Real visited [[Atlético Madrid]] in the first [[Madrid Derby]] of the season. Benzema gave Real the lead, but the game eventually ended in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/10/1-1-all-square-in-the-first-derby-of-the-season|title=1–1: All square in the first derby of the season|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=4 October 2015|access-date=4 October 2015}}</ref>

Ronaldo became the all-time top goalscorer for Real Madrid with a goal in a 3–0 victory over [[Levante UD|Levante]]. Marcelo and [[Jesé]] scored the other goals.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/10/3-0-real-madrid-go-provisionally-top-with-comprehensive-win-over-levante|title=3–0: Real Madrid go provisionally top with comprehensive win over Levante|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=17 October 2015|access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref>

Madrid faced [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] in the Champions League and came away with a goalless draw,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/10/0-0-all-square-in-paris-and-real-madrid-stay-top|title=0–0: All square in Paris and Real Madrid stay top|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=21 October 2015|access-date=21 October 2015}}</ref> which extended Madrid's unbeaten group stage streak to three years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/cs/Satellite?c=RM_Noticia_FA&cid=1330363715351&pagename=RealMadridResponsive%2FRM_Noticia_FA%2FRM_Noticia|title=Three years unbeaten in the group stage|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=21 October 2015|access-date=21 October 2015}}</ref>

In the top match of the ninth round, against [[Celta de Vigo]], Madrid came away with a 3–1 victory, thanks to goals from Ronaldo, Marcelo and Danilo (his first goal as a Real player).<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/10/1-3-la-liga-leaders-win-in-vigo|title=1–3: La Liga leaders win in Vigo|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=24 October 2015|access-date=21 October 2015}}</ref>

Goals from [[Isco]], Ronaldo and Jesé wrapped up the month of October up with a 3–1 home win over [[UD Las Palmas|Las Palmas]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/10/3-1-madrid-hold-firm-at-top-of-table|title=3–1: Madrid hold firm at top of table|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=31 October 2015|access-date=31 October 2015}}</ref>

===November===
The new month started with a narrow 1–0 victory against Paris Saint-Germain, which saw Madrid going through to the round of 16 in the Champions League. The lone goal was scored by [[Nacho (footballer, born 1990)|Nacho]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/11/1-0-madrid-qualify-for-champions-league-round-of-16|title=1–0: Madrid qualify for Champions League round of 16|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=3 November 2015|access-date=3 November 2015}}</ref>

Goals from Sergio Ramos and James Rodríguez were not enough to get by [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]], as Real lost the game 2–3 and the lead position of the table.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/11/3-2-real-madrid-slip-up-in-the-sanchez-pizjuan|title=3–2: Real Madrid slip up in the Sánchez Pizjuán|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=8 November 2015|access-date=8 November 2015}}</ref>

After the international break, Real hosted arch-rivals [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and suffered a 0–4 loss, their heaviest defeat in ''[[El Clásico]]'' since 0–5 in [[2010–11 Real Madrid CF season#Matches|2010]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/11/0-4-barcelona-win-the-clasico|title=0–4: Barcelona win the Clásico|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=21 November 2015|access-date=21 November 2015}}</ref>

Madrid secured the top spot of their Champions League group by defeating Shakhtar Donetsk 4–3 away after a brace from Ronaldo and goals from [[Luka Modrić]] and Dani Carvajal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/11/3-4-real-madrid-ensure-progress-to-last-16-as-group-winners|title=3–4: Real Madrid ensure progress to last 16 as group winners|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=25 November 2015|access-date=25 November 2015}}</ref>

Goals from Bale and Ronaldo snapped a two-game losing streak in the league with a 2–0 victory over [[SD Eibar|Eibar]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/11/0-2-bale-and-cristiano-ronaldo-seal-victory-in-ipurua|title=0–2: Bale and Cristiano Ronaldo seal victory in Ipurua|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=29 November 2015|access-date=29 November 2015}}</ref>

===December===
Madrid started the [[2015–16 Copa del Rey|cup season]] in style with a 3–1 victory over [[Cádiz CF|Cádiz]] by a brace from Isco and a goal from [[Denis Cheryshev]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/12/1-3-real-madrid-beat-cadiz-at-the-estadio-ramon-de-carranza|title=1–3: Real Madrid beat Cádiz at the Estadio Ramón de Carranza|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=2 December 2015|access-date=2 December 2015}}</ref> The match was controversial, however, as Cheryshev was suspended from the previous season for three yellow cards (when he was representing a different team) but played the game nonetheless due to an accounting mistake by Madrid officials. Real was disqualified from the 2015–16 Copa del Rey two days later by the competition judge despite protests from President Pérez.<ref name="mr 1">{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2015/12/02/futbol/equipos/real_madrid/1449092541.html|title=Alineación indebida de Cheryshev|trans-title=Unlawful Cheryshev appearance|newspaper=Marca|first=José Félix|last=Díaz|language=es|date=2 December 2015|access-date=2 December 2015}}</ref><ref name="mr 2">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35001655|title=Real Madrid not told Cheryshev was ineligible, says president|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=4 December 2015|access-date=4 December 2015}}</ref><ref name="DQ">{{cite news|url=http://cdn1.sefutbol.com/sites/default/files/sanciones/resolucion_1-16_ida_copa_rey_cadiz-r.madrid.pdf|title=Acuerdos y resolución del Juez de Competición acerca de los 1/16 de final de Copa de SM El Rey|publisher=rfef.es|date=4 December 2015|access-date=4 December 2015|archive-date=6 December 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151206154836/http://cdn1.sefutbol.com/sites/default/files/sanciones/resolucion_1-16_ida_copa_rey_cadiz-r.madrid.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref>

A brace from Benzema and goals from Bale and Ronaldo gave Madrid the edge to win 4–1 over [[Getafe CF|Getafe]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/12/4-1-bbc-with-the-goals-as-real-madrid-thrash-getafe|title=4–1: BBC with the goals as Real Madrid thrash Getafe|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=5 December 2015|access-date=5 December 2015}}</ref>

Four goals from Ronaldo, a hat-trick from Benzema and a goal from Mateo Kovačić wrapped up the group stage of the Champions League with an 8–0 thrashing of [[Malmö FF]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/12/8-0-ronaldo-and-benzema-to-the-fore-in-historic-rout-of-malmo|title=8–0: Ronaldo and Benzema to the fore in historic rout of Malmö|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=8 December 2015|access-date=8 December 2015}}</ref>

Madrid had a rough awakening back in La Liga when they lost 0–1 at [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/12/1-0-real-madrid-deserved-more-at-el-madrigal|title=1–0: Real Madrid deserved more at El Madrigal|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=13 December 2015|access-date=13 December 2015}}</ref>

A record 10–2 victory over [[Rayo Vallecano]] helped Madrid to secure the third spot in the league table with four goals from Bale, a hat-trick from Benzema, a Ronaldo brace and a goal from Danilo.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/12/10-2-real-madrid-relentless-as-bale-gets-four-goals|title=10–2: Real Madrid relentless as Bale gets four goals|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=20 December 2015|access-date=20 December 2015}}</ref>

Two goals from Ronaldo and a goal from Lucas Vázquez secured a 3–1 victory against Real Sociedad to end the year.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2015/12/3-1-real-madrid-sign-off-2015-with-victory|title=3–1: Real Madrid sign off 2015 with victory|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=30 December 2015|access-date=30 December 2015}}</ref>

===January===
The new year started with a 2–2 draw at [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], with goals from Benzema and Bale securing ten-man Real Madrid a point.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/2-2-ten-man-real-madrid-come-away-from-mestalla-with-draw|title=2–2: Ten-man Real Madrid come away from Mestalla with draw|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=3 January 2016|access-date=3 January 2016}}</ref>

On 4 January 2016, it was announced that Benítez was sacked and replaced by former assistant coach [[Zinedine Zidane]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/zidane-a-club-legend-in-the-real-madrid-dugout|title=Zidane: a club legend in the Real Madrid dugout|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=4 January 2016|access-date=4 January 2016}}</ref>

The Zidane era was opened with a 5–0 win over [[Deportivo de La Coruña]] thanks to a hat-trick by Bale and a brace from Benzema.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/5-0-a-hat-trick-from-bale-on-zidanes-debut|title=5–0: A hat trick from Bale on Zidane's debut|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=9 January 2016|access-date=9 January 2016}}</ref>

Gijón was defeated 5–1 with braces from Ronaldo and Benzema plus a Bale goal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/5-1-real-madrid-offer-goal-fest-with-masterclass-from-the-bbc|title=5–1: Real Madrid offer goal fest with masterclass from the BBC|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=17 January 2016|access-date=17 January 2016}}</ref>

A goal from Benzema was not enough in a 1–1 draw against Betis.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/1-1--real-madrid-deserved-to-win-at-the-benito-villamarin|title=1–1: Real Madrid deserved to win at the Benito Villamarín|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=24 January 2016|access-date=24 January 2016}}</ref>

Real then thrashed Espanyol 6–0, with Ronaldo scoring a hat-trick and Rodríguez and Benzema adding the other goals alongside an own goal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/01/6-0-real-madrid-hand-out-a-thrashing-as-cristiano-ronaldo-nets-a-hat-trick|title=6–0: Real Madrid hand out a thrashing as Cristiano Ronaldo nets a hat-trick|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=31 January 2016|access-date=31 January 2016}}</ref>

===February===
Cheryshev was loaned out to Valencia on 1 February 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/02/official-announcement-cheryshev|title=Official Announcement: Cheryshev|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=1 February 2016|access-date=1 February 2016}}</ref>

A late goal from Modrić saved the 2–1 win against Granada, after Benzema opened the scoring.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/02/1-2-a-fantastic-modric-goal-hands-whites-win-in-granada|title=1–2: A fantastic Modric goal hands Whites win in Granada|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=8 February 2016|access-date=8 February 2016}}</ref>

A stunning Ronaldo brace and goals from Rodríguez and [[Toni Kroos]] gave Madrid a 4–2 victory over Athletic Bilbao.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/02/4-2-real-madrid-beat-athletic-and-now-begin-thinking-about-roma|title=4–2: Real Madrid beat Athletic and now begin thinking about Roma|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|date=13 February 2016|access-date=13 February 2016}}</ref>

Against [[A.S. Roma|Roma]] in the Champions League round of 16, Ronaldo and Jesé helped Zidane to get off to a good start in Europe with a 2–0 win.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/02/0-2-madrid-move-closer-to-quarter-finals-of-champions-league|title=0–2: Madrid move closer to quarter-finals of Champions League|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref>

Madrid managed to get a 1–1 draw at Málaga with the goal being scored by Ronaldo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/02/1-1-real-madrid-draw-at-la-rosaleda|title=1–1: Real Madrid draw at La Roseleda|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=21 February 2016}}</ref>

The home match against Atlético Madrid was lost 0–1 and the gap to the first place blew to 12 points.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/02/0-1-atletico-win-the-derby|title=0–1: Atlético win the derby|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=27 February 2016}}</ref>

===March===
The new month was opened with a 3–1 victory at Levante with goals from Ronaldo, Isco and an own goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/03/1-3-victory-at-ciudad-de-valencia-stadium|title=1–3: Victory at Ciudad de Valencia Stadium|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=2 March 2016}}</ref>

Four goals from Ronaldo and goals by Pepe, Bale and Jesé secured Madrid a 7–1 win over Celta Vigo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/03/7-1-real-madrid-hand-out-thrashing-with-four-goals-from-cristiano-ronaldo|title=7–1: Real Madrid hand out thrashing with four goals from Cristiano Ronaldo|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=5 March 2016}}</ref>

Goals from Ronaldo and Rodríguez secured a 2–0 win over Roma (aggregate 4–0) in the round of 16 of the Champions League.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/03/2-0-a-win-to-seal-qualification-for-the-champions-league-quarter-finals|title=2–0: A win to seal qualification for the Champions League quarter-finals|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=8 March 2016}}</ref>

Casemiro secured the three points with a late goal, after Ramos initially put Madrid in the lead in a 2–1 away victory over Las Palmas.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/03/1-2-set-pieces-gave-the-victory-to-real-madrid-in-las-palmas|title=1–2: Set pieces gave the victory to Real Madrid in Las Palmas|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=13 March 2016}}</ref>

Goals from Benzema, Ronaldo, Bale and Jesé secured ''Los Blancos'' a 4–0 win over Sevilla.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/03/4-0-goals-against-sevilla-before-the-international-break|title=4–0: Goals against Sevilla before the international break|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=20 March 2016}}</ref>

===April===
''El Clásico'' was won 2–1 away from home with a Benzema equalizer and a late winner from Ronaldo, snapping Barcelona's 39-game unbeaten streak.<ref>[https://www.espn.com/soccer/blog/five-aside/77/post/2842799/real-madrid-win-clasico-and-end-barcelona-unbeaten-run Real Madrid end a Clasico winless streak and Barcelona's unbeaten run]</ref> Madrid outplayed Barça despite having been reduced to ten men in the second half with a Ramos red card and having a goal ruled out for a non-existent foul by Bale.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/1-2-real-madrid-come-from-behind-to-win-the-clasico-with-ten-men|title=1–2: Real Madrid come from behind to win the Clásico with ten men|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=2 April 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/35928630|title=Barcelona 1–2 Real Madrid|work=Mike Whalley|publisher=BBC Sport|date=2 April 2016}}</ref>

The first leg of the quarter-finals at the Champions League was lost 0–2 away from home to [[VfL Wolfsburg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/2-0-time-for-a-bernabeu-comeback|title=2–0: Time for a Bernabéu comeback|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=6 April 2016}}</ref>

Against Eibar, Madrid was able to get a 4–0 victory with goals from Rodríguez, Vázquez, Ronaldo and Jesé.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/4-0-real-madrid-orchestrate-a-goal-fest-against-eibar-and-now-turn-their-attentions-to-wolfsburg|title=4–0: Real Madrid orchestrate a goal fest against Éibar and now turn their attentions to Wolfsburg|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=9 April 2016}}</ref>

In the penultimate home leg against Wolfsburg, a perfect Ronaldo hat-trick propelled Madrid's comeback, with a 3–0 home win overturning the deficit and ensuring a 3–2 aggregate victory. The result also signified Madrid's progression to their sixth consecutive Champions League semi-finals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/3-0-comeback-accomplished-now-for-the-semi-finals|title=3–0: Comeback accomplished, now for the semi-finals|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=12 April 2016}}</ref>

Benzema, Isco, Bale, Rodríguez and Ronaldo gave Madrid a 5–1 victory at Getafe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/1-5-goal-fest-in-getafe-to-go-one-point-off-the-top|title=1–5: Goal fest in Getafe to go one point off the top|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=16 April 2016}}</ref>

Goals from Benzema, Vázquez and Modrić secured Madrid the three points in a 3–0 win over Villarreal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/3-0-real-madrid-keep-up-the-race-for-la-liga|title=3–0: Real Madrid keep up the race for La Liga|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=20 April 2016}}</ref>

A brace from Bale and a goal from Vázquez gave Madrid a comeback win against Rayo Vallecano after being down 0–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/2-3-real-madrid-bounce-and-are-provisional-league-leaders|title=2–3: Real Madrid produce a comeback to remain in the La Liga title race|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=23 April 2016}}</ref>

The first leg of the Champions League semi-finals against [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] ended in a goalless draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/0-0-final-berth-to-be-decided-at-the-bernabeu|title=0–0: Final berth to be decided at the Bernabéu|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref>

A Bale header gave Madrid a late 1–0 win at Real Sociedad.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/04/0-1-bale-goal-keeps-madrid-in-the-fight-for-la-liga|title=0–1: Bale goal keeps Madrid in the fight for La Liga|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref>

===May===
[[File:Sergio Ramos desde el balcón de la Presidencia de la Comunidad de Madrid con la Úndecima Copa de Europa.jpg|thumb|left|150px]]
[[Fernando (footballer, born 1987)|Fernando]] scored an own goal as Real Madrid progressed into the [[2016 UEFA Champions League Final|Champions League final]] by beating Manchester City, setting up a clash with neighbours Atlético Madrid for the second time in three years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/05/1-0-all-out-for-the-eleventh-european-cup-title-in-milan|title=1–0: All out for the eleventh European Cup title in Milan!|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=4 May 2016}}</ref>

A brace from Ronaldo and a goal from Benzema secured a narrow 3–2 win over Valencia. With this win, Real moved up to second place in the league with a chance at the title.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/05/3-2-real-madrid-win-and-la-liga-will-be-decided-on-the-final-match-day|title=3–2: Real Madrid win and La Liga will be decided on the final match day|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=8 May 2016}}</ref>

Madrid won 2–0 at Deportivo thanks to another Ronaldo brace at the last matchday of the league. With that win, Madrid finished second, just a point behind league champions Barcelona, also producing a remarkable 12-game winning streak to conclude the campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/05/0-2-madrid-conclude-the-la-liga-campaign-with-a-win-and-turn-their-attentions-to-the-champions-league-final|title=0–2: Madrid conclude the La Liga campaign with a win and turn their attentions to the Champions League final|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=14 May 2016}}</ref>

On 28 May 2016, Real Madrid's 11th Champions League title was won thanks to a 5–3 penalty shoot-out victory over Atlético Madrid after a 1–1 draw, with Ramos giving Real an early lead.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2016/05/1-1-la-undecima-is-ours|title=1–1: La Undécima is ours|publisher=Real Madrid C.F.|access-date=28 May 2016}}</ref>
