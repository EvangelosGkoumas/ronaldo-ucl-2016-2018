The 2017–18 season was [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid Club de Fútbol]]'s [[List of Real Madrid C.F. seasons|114th season]] in existence and the club's [[La Liga records and statistics#All-time table|87th consecutive season]] in the top flight of [[Football in Spain|Spanish football]]. It covered a period from 1 July 2017 to 30 June 2018.

Despite finishing a distant third in the [[2017–18 La Liga|league]] that season, Real Madrid won four trophies out of six possible, including a third consecutive [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] title, which made them the first team to achieve the feat since [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in [[1975–76 European Cup|1976]] and the first ever to do it twice. This was also Madrid's fourth European Cup in five years, and their 13th overall. The [[three-peat]] winning manager [[Zinedine Zidane]] resigned shortly after the season concluded; he would return by the end of [[2018–19 Real Madrid CF season#March|next season]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44315700|title=Zinedine Zidane: Real Madrid boss stands down five days after Champions League win|date=31 May 2018|work=BBC Sport|access-date=14 June 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref> Additionally, this was notably considered being [[Cristiano Ronaldo]]'s final time staying with Los Merengues after 9 years spent winning 16 trophies altogether.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/soccer-transfers/story/3560356/cristiano-ronaldo-leaves-real-madrid-to-join-juventus|title=Ronaldo Ronaldo: Cristiano Ronaldo leaves Real Madrid to sign with Juventus|date=10 Jul 2018|work=ESPN|access-date=10 Jul 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref>

This season was the first since [[2006–07 Real Madrid CF season|2006–07]] without [[Pepe (footballer, born 1983)|Pepe]], who departed to Turkish club [[Beşiktaş J.K.|Besiktas]].

===August===
On 8 August 2017, Madrid won the [[2017 UEFA Super Cup]], beating [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] 2–1 with goals from [[Casemiro]] and [[Isco]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/2-1-european-super-cup-champions|title=2-1: European Super Cup Champions!|publisher=Real Madrid|date=8 August 2017|access-date=8 August 2017}}</ref>

On 13 August 2017, Madrid won the away leg of the [[2017 Supercopa de España]], defeating [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] 3–1 with goals from [[Cristiano Ronaldo]], [[Marco Asensio]] and an own goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/1-3-a-real-madrid-exhibition-at-the-camp-nou|title=1-3: A Real Madrid exhibition at the Camp Nou|publisher=Real Madrid|date=13 August 2017|access-date=13 August 2017}}</ref> The match was controversial, as several referring mistakes affected the teams. First, Barcelona was awarded a questionable penalty on [[Luis Suárez]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/news/2017-2018/social-networks-leo-messi-and-luis-suarez-call-for-optimism|title=Leo Messi and Luis Suárez call for optimism|publisher=FCBarcelona.com|date=14 August 2017|access-date=16 August 2017}}</ref> which [[Lionel Messi|Messi]] converted to equalize in the 77th minute,<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2017/aug/13/barcelona-v-real-madrid-spanish-super-cup-first-leg-live|title= Barcelona 1-3 Real Madrid: Spanish Super Cup, first leg – as it happened|date=13 August 2017|work=Guardian|access-date=24 August 2017}}</ref> and then, after Real scored their second via substitute Ronaldo,<ref>{{Cite web|date=13 August 2017|title=Barcelona vs. Real Madrid - Football Match Report|url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/report?gameId=479398|access-date=15 October 2017|website=ESPN}}</ref> who shot into the top right corner from the edge of the box in the 80th minute, Cristiano was booked for removing his shirt as part of the celebration; two minutes later, he was booked again by the referee for allegedly diving when he collided with [[Samuel Umtiti]] in the box, which resulted in a second yellow card and Ronaldo being [[Fouls and misconduct (association football)#Red card (dismissal)|sent off]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-08-14|title=Barcelona 1 - 3 Real Madrid: as it happened, goals, match report|url=https://en.as.com/en/2017/08/13/football/1502613292_718396.html|access-date=2020-10-15|website=AS.com|language=en}}</ref> therefore getting suspended for the second leg. He was later given a five-match suspension after replays showed him push the referee in frustration for the red card. The latter penalty in hindsight derailed Madrid's season start and cost them points in La Liga. Real's last goal was scored in the 90th minute in similar fashion to the second, as Asensio shot into the top left corner following an assist from [[Lucas Vázquez]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/news/2017-2018/match-report-fc-barcelona-1-3-real-madrid-to-be-continued... |publisher=FCBarcelona.com |title=FC Barcelona 1-3 Real Madrid: To be continued... |date=13 August 2017 |access-date=13 August 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171029152757/https://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/news/2017-2018/match-report-fc-barcelona-1-3-real-madrid-to-be-continued... |archive-date=29 October 2017 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/1-3-a-real-madrid-exhibition-at-the-camp-nou|title=1-3: A Real Madrid exhibition at the Camp Nou|publisher=Real Madrid|date=13 August 2017|access-date=13 August 2017}}</ref>

Three days later, on 16 August 2017, Madrid clinched the title, after a 2–0 home win (5–1 on aggregate) with goals from Asensio and [[Karim Benzema]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/2-0-spanish-super-cup-champions|title=2-0: Spanish Super Cup Champions!|publisher=Real Madrid|date=17 August 2017|access-date=17 August 2017}}</ref> Goals from [[Gareth Bale]], Casemiro and [[Toni Kroos]] gave Madrid a 3–0 winning start in the [[2017–18 La Liga|league campaign]] at [[Deportivo de La Coruña|Deportivo La Coruña]] on 20 August 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/0-3-handsome-victory-and-top-spot-in-laliga-opener|title=0-3: Handsome victory and top spot in LaLiga opener|publisher=Real Madrid|date=20 August 2017|access-date=20 August 2017}}</ref> On 24 August 2017, the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League group stage]] draw was held, and Madrid was drawn in Group H, along with [[Borussia Dortmund]], [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and [[APOEL FC|APOEL]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/real-madrid-will-play-borussia-dortmund-tottenham-and-apoel-in-group-h-of-the-champions-league|title=Real Madrid will play Borussia Dortmund, Tottenham and APOEL in Group H of the Champions League|publisher=Real Madrid|date=20 August 2017|access-date=20 August 2017}}</ref> On 27 August 2017, two goals from Asensio were not enough as Madrid drew [[Valencia CF|Valencia]] 2–2 at the Bernabéu.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2017/08/2-2-madrid-sought-victory-until-the-end|title=2-2: Madrid sought victory until the end|publisher=Real Madrid|date=27 August 2017|access-date=27 August 2017}}</ref>

gave Madrid a 1–0 advantage after the first leg of the Copa del Rey quarter-finals against [[CD Leganés|Leganés]] on 18 January 2018.<ref>{{cite ===February===
On 3 February 2018, Madrid travelled to Levante and came away with a 2–2 draw, despite having been in front twice after goals from Ramos and Isco.<ref>{{cite 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> Against Leganés, on 21 February 2018, Madrid won 3–1 after goals from Vázquez, Casemiro and Ramos.<ref>{{cite ===March===
On 3 March 2018, a brace from Ronaldo and a goal from Bale helped Madrid to get a 3–1 win over Getafe.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/03/3-1-victory-over-getafe-as-attentions-turn-to-the-champions-league|title=3-1: Victory over Getafe as attentions turn to the Champions League|date=3 March 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> Three days later, the return leg of the UEFA Champions League round of 16 tie against Paris Saint-Germain was won 2–1 after goals from Ronaldo and Casemiro. That result gave Madrid a 5–2 advantage over the two legs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/03/1-2-the-european-champions-impose-their-rule-in-paris|title=1-2: The European champions impose their rule in Paris|date=6 March 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> Another four days later, a brace from Ronaldo secured Real all three points in a 2–1 away win at Eibar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/03/1-2-cristiano-ronaldo-claims-the-winner-at-ipurua|title=1-2: Cristiano Ronaldo claims the winner at Ipurua|date=10 March 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> On 18 March 2018, Madrid defeated Girona 6–3 at home with four goals from Ronaldo and singles from Vázquez and Bale.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/03/6-3-attacking-masterclass-sees-cristiano-ronaldo-hit-four|title=6-3: Attacking masterclass sees Cristiano Ronaldo hit four|date=18 March 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> On the last day of March, a brace from Bale and a goal from Benzema helped Real to record a fifth consecutive victory with a 3–0 win at Las Palmas.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/03/0-3-victory-in-las-palmas-ahead-of-champions-league-clash|title=0-3: Victory in Las Palmas ahead of Champions League clash|date=31 March 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref>

===April===
On 3 April 2018, in the Champions League quarter-finals against [[Juventus FC|Juventus]], Ronaldo scored twice, including a spectacular overhead kick, and Marcelo added another goal to give Madrid a 3–0 first leg victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/0-3-ronaldo-stars-as-real-madrid-thump-juventus|title=0-3: Ronaldo stars as Real Madrid thump Juventus|date=3 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> Five days later, a volley goal from Ronaldo put Madrid in the lead against Atlético, but the match eventually ended 1–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/1-1-oblak-and-the-woodwork-deny-real-madrid-victory|title=1-1: Oblak and the woodwork deny Real Madrid victory|date=8 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> On 11 April 2018, down 0–3, Ronaldo converted an injury time penalty to secure Madrid a spot in the semi-finals of the Champions League; the 1–3 loss was enough as Real won 4–3 on aggregate against Juventus.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/1-3-into-the-semis-after-weathering-the-storm|title=1-3: Into the semis after weathering the storm|date=11 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> Four days later, goals from Isco and Casemiro secured a 2–1 win over Málaga.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/1-2-isco-proves-decisive-in-the-win-against-malaga|title=1-2: Isco proves decisive in the win against Málaga|date=15 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> A late Ronaldo equalizer got Madrid a 1–1 draw against Athletic Bilbao on 18 April 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/1-1-real-madrid-deserve-more-against-athletic|title=1-1: Real Madrid deserve more against Athletic|date=18 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> A week later, against [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in the semi-finals of the Champions League, Madrid got a 2–1 away leg win after goals from Marcelo and Asensio.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/1-2-real-madrid-strike-back-to-beat-bayern-in-munich|title=1-2: Real Madrid strike back to beat Bayern in Munich|date=25 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref> On 28 April 2018, the game against Leganés was won 2–1 with goals from Bale and Mayoral.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/04/2-1-bale-and-mayoral-secure-all-three-points-against-leganes|title=2-1: Bale and Mayoral secure all three points against Leganés|date=28 April 2018|work=realmadrid.com}}</ref>

===May===
A brace from Benzema against Bayern Munich helped Madrid to reach the [[2018 UEFA Champions League Final|final]] after the second leg ended in a 2–2 draw on 1 May 2018, which secured a 4–3 aggregate victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/05/2-2-all-out-for-the-decimotercera-in-kiev|title=2-2: All out for the Decimotercera in Kiev!|date=1 May 2018|website=Realmadrid.com}}</ref> The ''Clásico'', five days later, ended in a 2–2 draw with goals from Ronaldo and Bale.<ref>{{cite Just three days later, on 9 May 2018, Madrid lost 2–3 at Sevilla with late goals from Mayoral and Ramos.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/05/3-2-madrid-slip-up-at-the-sanchez-pizjuan|title=3-2: Madrid slip up at the Sánchez-Pizjuán|date=9 May 2018|website=Realmadrid.com}}</ref> Three days later, Madrid won 6–0 against Celta Vigo, with Bale scoring a brace and Isco, Hakimi, Kroos and an own goal chipping in the other goals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/05/6-0-the-bernabeu-enjoys-an-attacking-exhibition-in-the-final-home-game-of-the-season|title=6-0: The Bernabéu enjoys an attacking exhibition in the final home game of the season|date=12 May 2018|website=Realmadrid.com}}</ref> The last game of the league season, on 19 May 2018, ended in a 2–2 away draw against Villarreal, after goals from Bale and Ronaldo initially gave Madrid the lead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/05/2-2-real-madrid-finish-off-their-laliga-season-with-a-draw-against-villarreal|title=2-2: Real Madrid finish off their LaLiga season with a draw against Villarreal|date=19 May 2018|website=Realmadrid.com}}</ref> On 26 May 2018, two goals from Bale and another one from Benzema helped Madrid to win their third consecutive Champions League title, and fourth in five years, after defeating [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realmadrid.com/en/news/2018/05/3-1-madrid-clinch-la-decimotercera|title=3-1: Madrid clinch La Decimotercera|date=26 May 2018|website=Realmadrid.com}}</ref>
